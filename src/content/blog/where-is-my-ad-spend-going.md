If you have ever handed a few thousand dollars to an agency or boosted a post and then quietly wondered, *"did that actually do anything?"*, you are not alone. It is the single most common frustration we hear from small business owners: money goes out, a vague report comes back, and nobody can tell you what it bought.

Here is the good news. You do not need to be a data analyst to know where your ad spend goes. You need three things set up correctly.

## 1. Conversion tracking that actually fires

A "conversion" is any action worth money to you: a form fill, a phone call, a booking, a purchase. If these are not tracked properly, every report after that point is fiction.

Make sure each meaningful action sends a conversion event to your ad platforms and to GA4. Then spot-check it. Submit a test lead and confirm it shows up. You would be amazed how many accounts are quietly mistracking the one number that matters.

## 2. One dashboard, not five tabs

Your Google Ads dashboard says one thing, Meta says another, and your sales spreadsheet says a third. The fix is a single view that ties **spend** to **conversions** to **revenue**, broken out by channel.

When it is set up right, you can answer the only questions that matter at a glance:

- How much did we spend on each channel?
- How many leads or sales did each one produce?
- What did it cost us per result, and what did we get back?

## 3. Attribution you can explain

Attribution just means deciding which channel gets credit when a customer touches more than one. You do not need a PhD-level model. You need a consistent, explainable one, plus a human who can tell you, in plain English, "Google search is driving most of your booked revenue; Facebook is filling the top of the funnel."

## The test for any agency or marketer

Ask one question: *"Can you show me where every dollar went last month, and what it produced?"* If the answer is a glossy report full of impressions and "reach" but no path from spend to revenue, keep looking.
