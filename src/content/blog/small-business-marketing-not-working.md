You are spending money on marketing every month, but you cannot point to what it actually bought you. You are not alone, and it is rarely because the channel is broken. After working with small businesses who came to us frustrated, the same five problems come up again and again.

## 1. You are measuring the wrong thing

Likes, impressions, and "reach" feel like progress, but they do not pay the bills. If you are not tracking leads, booked calls, and revenue, you are flying blind. Pick the two or three numbers that map to money and judge everything against them.

## 2. There is no tracking, so nobody knows what works

Most small businesses run ads and SEO with broken or missing tracking. When you cannot see which channel drove a sale, every budget decision becomes a guess. Clean conversion tracking is the unglamorous fix that makes everything else work.

## 3. You are spread too thin

Three channels run at 30 percent effort lose to one channel run well. If you are dabbling in ads, social, SEO, and email all at once with no clear priority, consolidate. Win one channel, then add the next.

## 4. Your website does not convert

You can buy all the traffic in the world, but if your site is slow, confusing, or unclear about what to do next, that traffic leaks away. Before spending more on ads, make sure the page they land on is built to convert.

## 5. You set it and forgot it

Marketing is not a slow cooker. Campaigns drift, costs creep, and what worked last quarter fades. The businesses that win review the numbers weekly and adjust, instead of checking in once a quarter.
